Bio

Born on International Children’s Day - 20 November[1], Tamzid Rahman is a Bangladeshi Social Entrepreneur & Health Activist known for his advocacy and actions towards his goal of a 0% mortality ratio due to lack of blood transfusion in Bangladesh[2].

At the age of 15, he founded his organization BloodLink Foundation, which is Bangladesh’s first and largest peer-to-peer blood donation app.[3] His app helps connect blood donors to recipients, online, completely free of cost.

Start to his Activism

Tamzid's activism began at the age of 14 when he got accepted at a high school entrepreneurship cohort called BeyondGrades, funded by EMK Center of U.S. Embassy Dhaka.[4] As part of the cohort, Tamzid pitched the idea of financial inclusion and independence of teenagers by involving national banks of the country.

Later in 2022, Tamzid received a $250 Mini-Grant from Peace First, which allowed him to establish The Plenty Project[5] where he organized a Mental Health Workshop at Dhaka's St. Joseph Higher Secondary School, hosting 150 student participants, alongside counselors and guest speakers. Further, he launched two awareness e-books - on menstrual hygiene and cyber safety for teenagers from this organization.

Success of BloodLink

Since its inception, Tamzid’s BloodLink App has directly contributed to the saving of over 500 children’s lives.

Tamzid’s app has over 400 downloads in the Play Store and they’ve so far received over 3500+ blood requests via the messenger chatbot extension.[6] So far his initiative has over 15,000+ individuals reaching, in general.

Tamzid has so far raised over $15,000 in grant funding with support from the ICT Division, the Government of Bangladesh, KidsRights Netherlands, The Pollination Project, and Project Nightfall Philanthropy.[7]

Honors & Awards

Tamzid won the Glocal Teen Hero Bangladesh 2023 organized by Global Pvt. Ltd.[8] He's also the winner of the Bangabandhu International Cyber Security Awareness Award 2023 by UNDP Bangladesh and ICT Ministry of Bangladesh Government, with a fund of USD 10,000.[9]
